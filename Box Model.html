The Dragon Boat Club at ASU

About

Dragon Boating originally starting in China 2,000 years ago from folk traditions.

Now an international sport, in 2009 the Dragon Boat Club at Arizona State University was founded. This sport is all about community and personal development.

It is a great way to stay active and committed even for older generations in other divisions. People from all different cultures bond over it.

Dragon Boat is a beginner friendly sport. The hard work that each member spends goes towards racing for festivals. The club gets the opportunity participate in many festivals like, San Diego, Long Beach, Santa Fe Dam, and Vegas. The Tempe Town Lake Dragon Boat Festival.

Info

Dragon Boat is a sport that you race against others. There are 20 people who paddle, and one person drumming, and the other person steering the boat. In total, that would be 22 people who would fit in a boat. Dragon Boat is an overall solid sport that can be a fun hobby or an extreme push for people that need community and Constancy in life.

What to Bring

Bring water, sunblock, and yourself! This is optional, but a hat and sunglasses are nice during morning practices. Paddling wise, life-jackets and paddles will be provided on site at the Tempe Town Lake Marina.

Location + Carpool

The Dragon Boat Club at ASU meets at the Tempe Town Lake Marina on time(start of practice).

Carpool is behind Two Hands on Apache Boulevard, provided by other members. So show up 15 minutes before practice. If anything changes, message the discord. Location Link
